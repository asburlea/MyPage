Our research group operates at the intersection of neural engineering, AI and machine learning, to model and decode human brain and behavioral activity.

We develop AI-driven brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) and neurotechnologies that integrate uncertainty quantification, multimodal sensory models, and refined interaction control strategies for robotics and BCIs.

Additionally, we explore the role of AI in higher education, enhancing digital skills and ethical understanding of neurotechnology. Our computational frameworks and neuroAI approaches aim to advance next-generation BCIs, restoring lost motor and cognitive functions in individuals with neurological disorders.
